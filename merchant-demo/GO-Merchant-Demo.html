Why merchants choose a halal-only marketplace

Mainstream delivery apps mix many cuisines; halal diners still worry about sourcing, certification, and menu clarity. GO is positioned as European halal retail & food — fewer irrelevant listings, sharper intent, clearer partner standards.

Customer promise

  • Verified halal partners only — certifications & menu checks before going live.
  • Categories spanning restaurants, grocery, butcher, bakery, snacks (where published).
  • WhatsApp-assisted ordering during rollout; fuller checkout & couriers on the roadmap.

What you gain

  • Brand alignment with halal trust, not generic “fast food” noise.
  • Structured catalogue tooling: machine-readable price lists (CSV) + optional documents.
  • Operational profile: identity, halal proofs, ops window, payout readiness fields.

Onboard A → Z (what the portal collects)

The partners.html experience is an end-to-end checklist: legal identity, category, structured pricing, halal proofs, operations, and payout readiness. Submissions create a merchant application in the GO backend for review (typically 2–3 business days).

Business profile

Trading name, city, category (restaurant, grocery, butcher, bakery, snacks…), contact email, supporting uploads.

Catalogue & prices

Machine-readable CSV price list (published pipeline) plus optional PDF/XLSX for validation during onboarding.

Halal compliance pack

Certificates, supplier letters, or internal controls — whatever matches your legal market & brand.

Operations & payouts

Prep times, handoff rules, service radius notes, and banking metadata captured for future settlement flows.

Halal trust is the product — not a marketing sticker

GO’s public FAQ states that only verified partners are listed, with certification and menu checks before launch. Alcohol, gambling, and plainly non-halal storefront categories stay out of this product surface.

Contracting

A dedicated partner agreement (contrato parceiro comerciante) is published for transparency. Executed copies, fee schedules, and jurisdiction details should be finalised with the HalalEat EU team before trading.

Data & documents

Upload slots accept PDFs, scans, and spreadsheets. Keep filenames meaningful (e.g. halal-cert-2026.pdf) to speed review.
